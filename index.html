About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, often described as the “Crown of Palaces,” is a breathtaking mausoleum built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Completed in 1653 after 22 years of meticulous craftsmanship, the Taj Mahal stands as an enduring symbol of eternal love.
This UNESCO World Heritage Site is renowned for its intricate carvings, symmetrical design, and the stunning interplay of light on its white marble structure. Nestled along the banks of the Yamuna River, it remains one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, drawing visitors from all corners of the globe.

History

The story of the Taj Mahal began in the early 17th century when the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan fell deeply in love with Mumtaz Mahal, his beloved wife. Mumtaz was not only his companion but also a trusted advisor and confidante. In 1631, she passed away while giving birth to their fourteenth child, leaving Shah Jahan heartbroken. He vowed to honor her memory with a grand monument, one that would symbolize the eternity of their love.
